Celebrating the Spirit of Cooperation

T he International Cooperative Alliance (ICA) proudly presents a global initiative that shines a light on the cultural roots and historical richness of the cooperative movement: the Cooperative Cultural Heritage Working Group (CCH-WG).

In a landmark recognition, UNESCO has added cooperatives to the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity, celebrating their enduring role in shaping inclusive, community-based economies across the globe. Co-operatives are now officially recognized among 16 new cultural elements safeguarding our shared human legacy.

Inspired by this global honor, the CCH-WG has encouraged their members to mark the culturally significant cooperative sites from across continents—each representing a powerful story of solidarity, resilience, and local heritage. These sites stand as living testaments to how cooperatives have shaped societies, empowered communities, and preserved cultural traditions.
